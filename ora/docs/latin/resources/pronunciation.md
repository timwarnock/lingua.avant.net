# <span tooltip="Pronuntiatio = Pronunciation">Pronuntiatio</span>

Latin remains the official language of the Roman Rite. Praying the Rosary in Latin connects one directly to centuries of tradition, to the See of Peter within the universal Church.

Many of the prayers have rhythmic and melodic qualities in Latin that aid memorization and prayerful meditation.

The Latin Rosary is especially used in monasteries, seminaries, and traditional communities worldwide.

---

## Phonemes

Ecclesiastical Latin pronunciation, known as the "more romano" or Roman style, is the standard for the liturgy and the Rosary in the Catholic Church. It developed from the spoken Latin of Italy in the medieval and Renaissance periods. The system features five pure vowels, each with a single, steady sound and none of the gliding diphthongs typical of English. In classical Latin, ae and oe were true diphthongs, but ecclesiastical usage simplifies them to the single vowel "eh," as heard in "saeculum" from the Gloria Patri. Combinations such as au are pronounced with two distinct vowels in one syllable, producing the clear, rhythmic flow that supports meditative prayer and chant.

!!! success "Vowels"
    + a like the a in "father"
    + e like the e in "bed"
    + i like the ee in "see"
    + o like the o in "for" (short)
    + u like the oo in "boot"

!!! success "Consonants"
    + c before e, i, ae, oe is "ch" (as in "church")
    + g before e, i is soft "j"
    + gn is like "ny" in "canyon"
    + ti before a vowel (not after s or x) is "tsee"
    + h is silent
    + qu is "kw"
    + x is "ks"
    + ae and oe are pronounced as "eh"

Stress usually falls on the second-to-last syllable. In prayer, use a clear and measured tone with reverence.
