# <span tooltip="Pronuntiatio = Pronunciation">Pronuntiatio</span>

Latin remains the official language of the Roman Rite. Praying the Rosary in Latin connects directly to centuries of tradition and the universal Church.

Many of the prayers have rhythmic and melodic qualities in Latin that aid memorization and meditation.

The Latin Rosary is especially used in monasteries, seminaries, and traditional communities worldwide.

## Vowels

Ecclesiastical Latin uses five pure vowels with no diphthongs as in English.

!!! success "Vowels"
    + a like the a in "father"
    + e like the e in "bed"
    + i like the ee in "see"
    + o like the o in "for" (short)
    + u like the oo in "boot"

## Consonants

Ecclesiastical Latin (used in the Church and for the Rosary) differs from classical Latin.

!!! success "Key consonant rules"
    + c before e, i, ae, oe is "ch" (as in "church")
    + g before e, i is soft "j"
    + gn is like "ny" in "canyon"
    + ti before a vowel (not after s or x) is "tsee"
    + h is silent
    + qu is "kw"
    + x is "ks"
    + ae and oe are pronounced as "eh"

## Stress and rhythm

Stress usually falls on the second-to-last syllable. In prayer, use a clear and measured tone with reverence.

The best practice is to listen repeatedly to native speakers or choirs praying the full Rosary. Each prayer page here includes audio for every passage.

## Recommendations

Listen to the Latin Rosary audio on the prayer pages here. Traditional monastic and choral recordings of the Rosary in Latin are also excellent for learning the natural flow and rhythm.

## Not Recommended

Classical Latin pronunciation is not used for the liturgy or the Rosary in the Church.
